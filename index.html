About Me

  I am an MSc Robotics student at the University of New South Wales. Over the past three years, I have participated in research projects, particularly working on the development related to computer vision and artificial intelligence.
  After I earned my bachelor's degree in Power Mechanical Engineering at National Tsing Hua University, I worked as a firmware engineer at PEGATRON Corporation. I participated in the UEFI development and devoted myself to problem-solving and boosting product performance.
  I worked on the research project CHARM: Companion Healthcare Aid Robot Manager with Prof. Li-Chen Fu at National Taiwan University from May 2021. I designed a user behaviour recognition system to detect abnormal behaviour of the elderly and reduce the risk of falls. The work has been awarded the 2021 National Innovation Award in Academic Research.
  In February 2022, I joined the Institute of Information Science at Academia Sinica. In the AI-Automated Training System for Figure Skating project, I worked on the automation of video alignment with Prof. Lun-Wei Ku . I developed the video alignment function with a self-supervised learning method to visualize the motion comparison between coach and learner.
  From these experiences, I have developed a passion for not only providing engineering solutions to various real-world problems but also being a contributor to novel robotics technologies.

Recent Work

Figure Skating Video Alignment

The function aligns videos from coach and learner. The model is trained from scratch based on a self-supervised method.

Social Companion Robot

The robot is designed for the elderly with dementia. It provides functions such as chatting, entertaining, health monitoring, stresses relieving, and telecare.

Robot Control and Path Planning

The project aims to control the 6-DoF robot arm for a specific task. This task is carried out utilizing the UR5e Robot Arm and controlled by MATLAB program.
